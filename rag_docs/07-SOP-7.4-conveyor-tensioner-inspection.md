## SOP-7.4 Conveyor Belt Tensioner Inspection, Assembly Line 5, Station 2

This procedure covers routine inspection of the drive belt tensioner on the main parts conveyor at Assembly Line 5, Station 2.

1. Stop the conveyor and lock out power per standard lockout/tagout procedure before inspection.
2. Check belt tension by applying moderate thumb pressure at the midpoint span — deflection should not exceed 12mm.
3. Inspect the tensioner spring for visible corrosion or cracking.
4. Check belt edges for fraying or uneven wear, which may indicate misalignment.
5. Record tension reading and visual inspection result in the Line 5 equipment log.
6. Release lockout and restart conveyor only after confirming the area is clear.

Safety note: Never adjust tension while the conveyor is powered. Two-person verification required before restart if any adjustment was made during inspection.

## SOP-7.4 Required Tools and Materials

- Tension gauge (Line 5 standard)
- Lockout/tagout kit
- Line 5 equipment inspection log
